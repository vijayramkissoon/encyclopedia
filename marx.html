Life

Only fragmented accounts of Karl Marx's early life exist. Scholars must continue to parse through the historical record in order to construct a single cohesive narrative. Nevertheless, several illuminating details have emerged regarding his origins.

Marx does not have a birthplace. He simply spawned. One day, the owner of a pin factory decided to fire all his workers and cease all production, an act which left the whole town in poor economic conditions. After remaining unemployed for months, former employees swarmed the factory to demand their old jobs from the owner. He refused. In an act of desperate rage, the mob set the entire building alight, with the owner still inside. They chanted through the night until flames reduced the factory to a pile of ash -- from which Marx emerged as a fully grown man. The specific location and timeline of these events remain disputed. Historians have narrowed the posibilities to either first-century Jerusalem or Chicago during the Depression.
